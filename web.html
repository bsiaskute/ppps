Key projects
01
Home page personalization
Implementing rules-based personalization on a large scale was one of my key objectives for the second half of my placement. Personalized content based on user profiles had never been implemented before. The project's aim was to deliver users with a specific bike profile content on the home page that would get accelerate their path towards the next key step in their user journey.
Show More
Together with the website manager and website coordinator, we outlined four key phases of the user journey (figure 2) from the Triumph attribution model and associated a key metric to them. Each phase would have a set of rules (criteria) of when and to whom the content would be shown. I selected the content based on the attribution model and created the home page variations. After adding translations, I created the rules sets and tested them, having to use workarounds around certain system limitations.

The concept and phases of the project are now being expanded to more bikes and applied to other sections of the website.

02
Data capture pop-ins
Reworking personalized pop-ins was a key project that set the groundwork for me to create more advanced personalized content. The project's aim was to target users with a specific bike profile and show them pop-ins with a message of signing up for email communicationss or taking a test ride.
Show More
To achieve this, the digital analyst and I created a rules path that followed simple yes/no question structure (figure 6, slide 3). The system would go along the specified path, asking itself whether the user met the criteria and show content based on the question answered “yes”. The rules path was created with streamlined maintenance and ensure consistency across the data capture and test drive pop-ins. Additionally, the targeting was set todivonly show pop-ins to users with an existing interest in the specific bike.

The rework turned pop-ins into a major lead generation mechanism (figure 6, slide 4) that required very little maintenance and investment but had good conversion due to effective targeting. The pop-ins also supported the launch of a Triumph brand newsletter (figure 6, slide 7). In figure 6 you can see a presentation deck I created and presented at the global marketing managers meeting.

03
A/B testing
Home page redesign
As part of the team's effort to increase traffic to high-conversion pages early in the journey, I redesigned the home page globally. However, to confirm that the new design was working as intended, I suggested and launched the first A/B test of a page design. This was a part of the team's future goals, to continuously optimize content. The result was a 4.7% increase in traffic from the new version of the home page to key conversion pages. Afterwards, I was asked to produce an A/B testing guide to leave for the team after my placement ended.

After I had redesigned and put live the Factory Visitor Experience website, the Customer Experience team asked to have the Triumph Adventure Experience pages redesigned as well. After presenting and getting approval from Head of Customer Experience, I built the new section and presented the new version as well. After the new version was live for a week, we already saw an increase in data capture forms.
